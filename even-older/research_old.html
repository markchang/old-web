Research and Publications

Mark's research focuses on FPGAs (Field Programmable Gate Arrays). FPGAs are hardware devices that can be programmed to implement almost any complex digital circuit. Mark's interests include application design for FPGAs, FPGA arithmetic, FPGA architectures, and computer-aided design tools. Mark is a firm believer that FPGAs will someday rule the electronics world.

More information can be found on Reconfigurable Systems Research Group web site.
