Notes:

    Three spectral images of the same coordinates at the same time. Histogram normalized for viewing. These pictures are of a subvisual contrail.
    Left:
    0.66um channel. Typical for many satellites and commonly used today for land surface classifications. Not visible.
    Right:
    11um channel. Dark is cold, light is warm. You can see the contrail, but it would be difficult to fully define its extent without the middle panel.
    Middle:
    1.88um channel. Near a strong water vapor absorption band, and, during the day, is extremely sensitive to the presence of high level clouds.
    Used together, we can get a decent cloud mask.
