Flexible Instruction Processors
FIPs are assembled by parameterized processor templates
Allows customized hardware to be exposed as new instructions
Critical resources can be adjusted according to domain requirements
Can implement same instruction set with different design tradeoffs
1.The processor and its associated op-codes provide a means to optimize control paths through optimizing compilers. Conventional ad-hoc sharing regimes are harder to optimize.
2.Resource management can even be done at run-time through the use of run-time reconfiguration.
3.Can enable high-level data structures to be easily supported in hardware.
