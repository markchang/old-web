Propagation engine:
   How do my variables and operations influence each other? In other words, how does my algorithm’s datapath grow and contract during the execution of the program?
   In manual precision analysis, such as in some of the hand mappings we’ve done in the past, in order to know how many bits of precision we need to allocate to an operation, we analyze the data flow up through that point and beyond.

Range finding / Data profiling:
   While the first step tells us how our data path may grow and contract purely from the operations done, the actual utilization of the data path may vary wildly from these bounds. Another way to gain insight into an algorithm is to run the algorithm and record the range of values that pass through variables and operators. While this is data-set dependent, it is beneficial in that it pinpoints areas where optimization beyond the ‘provable’ bounds of precision may yield benefits under acceptable error constraints.

Simulation:
   Now that we’ve determined the provable and run-time precision requirements of our algorithm and representative data sets, we can constrain variables and operators to a fixed precision and simulate the effects of that fixed-precision on the quality of our results. In this simulation, we find out how sensitive our algorithm is to variations in precision, and whether or not the precision choices we made will yield acceptable results.

User guidance / slack analysis:
   Given that we have nodes in our data path that we want to constrain to some fixed-precision, which nodes do I concentrate on first during my manual precision optimization?
   This usually is an iterative process. Developers can implement a non-optimal data path and iteratively constrain parts of the system to meet a constraint, such as getting the design to fit in a particular area constraint.
